HOSPITAL COURSE SUMMARY:
ADMISSION DIAGNOSES AND FINDINGS:
72 yo male admitted from ED with left-sided weakness, facial droop, and expressive aphasia. Initial CT head showed no acute hemorrhage. Vitals: BP 184/96, HR 88, Temp 37.1. Patient reported intermittent blurry vision in the right eye over the last 2 days. 

HOSPITAL COURSE:
Patient received IV tPA in the ED and was transferred to the neuro ICU. Left-sided weakness improved over 24 hours. Aphasia resolved completely by day 3. BP controlled with IV labetalol and transitioned to oral amlodipine. MRI brain confirmed a small acute ischemic infarct in the right MCA territory.

DISCHARGE DIAGNOSES AND STATUS:
1. Acute right MCA ischemic stroke - left-sided weakness significantly improved.
2. Hypertension - well controlled on amlodipine.
Discharge physical exam shows 4/5 strength in left upper and lower extremities. Facial droop has resolved. No acute distress. Patient cleared by PT/OT for discharge to acute rehab.
